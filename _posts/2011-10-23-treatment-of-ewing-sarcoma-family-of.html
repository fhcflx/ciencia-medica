Ewing sarcoma treatment is highly succesfull in non-metastatic patients, but the high-dose chemotherapy poses a significant risk of secondary leukemia. A group from Hospital Sant Joan de DÃ©u, Barcelona, Spain has published a small phase II single-arm non-randomized study reporting good results with a less toxic, modified chemotherapy protocol. Their results were as good as standard chemotherapy that uses higher cummulative doses, and they had no secondary disease in their group, after a median follow-up of more than 4 years! Relapses were seen in patients that did not receive radiotherapy, and they were late (more than 2 years after end of treatment). This report may represent a major advance in the treatment of non-metastatic Ewing sarcoma and should yield larger studies to test the feasibility of this lower-exposure chemotherapy approach.
